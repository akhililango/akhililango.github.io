Work In Progress

Ad Auction With Consumer Search
Job market paper
Abstract
Working paper

I develop a theory of digital marketplaces where an intermediary provides a platform for ﬁrms to list their product and for consumers to search. The intermediary determines the order in which products are displayed by con- ducting an auction. The effect of the auction on product prices is captured by the auction reserve price, which is chosen by the intermediary to maximise its commission from ﬁrms. A ﬁrm higher in the list charges a higher price for its product and earns a larger proﬁt. These results are in contrast with those from the standard models of prominence where prices are hidden prior to search. In the future, I plan to combine insights from consumer-ﬁrm and ﬁrm- intermediary interactions to characterise price dispersion and competition in the overall market equilibrium. Further, I will use this framework to analyse counterfactual scenarios to understand the implications for consumer welfare and market efﬁciency.
